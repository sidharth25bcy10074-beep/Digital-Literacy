# digital literacy project
TASK 1:

Digital Literacy Awareness Infographic was created using Canva, a free and user-friendly design platform. The infographic focuses on three key aspects: understanding digital literacy, safe internet practices, and maintaining a professional online presence. It explains digital literacy as the ability to effectively and responsibly use digital technologies for communication, learning, and collaboration.

The design highlights important tools for students such as Google Docs, GitHub, and LinkedIn, along with safety tips like using strong passwords, avoiding suspicious links, and protecting personal information. Additionally, it emphasizes the importance of maintaining a professional digital identity, especially on platforms that are visible to recruiters.

One interesting aspect of creating the infographic was learning how to present complex ideas in a visually simple and engaging way. The most challenging part was selecting the right layout and balancing text with visuals so that the content remains clear without overcrowding the design. Overall, this task improved my ability to communicate information effectively through visuals.

TASK 4: 

Poor digital communication can lead to serious misunderstandings. For example, a student once sent a message to a professor using informal language and unclear wording while requesting a deadline extension. The message lacked proper greeting, explanation, and respect, which made the professor perceive it as rude and unprofessional. As a result, the request was denied. This situation could have been avoided by following proper email etiquette, such as using a clear subject line, polite tone, and structured format. Providing a valid reason and showing respect would have increased the chances of approval. This highlights the importance of clear, professional, and respectful communication in academic and professional environments.

TASK 5:

While researching UPI fraud, what surprised me the most was how easily people are tricked into authorizing payments themselves. Many victims are not hacked technically but are manipulated psychologically through urgency and fear. I realized that even educated users can fall into such traps if they are not alert.

One habit I will personally change is being more cautious before approving any payment request. I will always double-check the purpose of a transaction and never act in haste, even if the message seems urgent. Additionally, I will educate my family members about common scams, especially the fact that a UPI PIN is never required to receive money. This awareness can help prevent financial losses and promote safer digital practices.
